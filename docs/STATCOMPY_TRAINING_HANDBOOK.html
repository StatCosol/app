Use this handbook in live training, induction batches, and role refresher sessions. Each module includes the normal execution flow, the exception flow, and the next-team handoff.

How to Use This Handbook

For Users

  1. Understand your dashboard and menu.
  2. Follow the step-by-step module process.
  3. Use the exception and handoff notes when work is blocked.

For Trainers

  1. Explain role purpose first.
  2. Demonstrate the normal daily cycle.
  3. Show one rejection or escalation scenario.
  4. End with a hands-on exercise.

Common Login Flow

  1. Open the relevant portal URL.
  2. Enter the registered email ID.
  3. Enter the password.
  4. Click Sign In.
  5. Confirm the correct role dashboard opens.
  6. Use the left menu to navigate to module pages.

End-to-End Platform Flow

Setup and Ownership

  1. Admin creates client, users, branches, and assignments.
  2. Client and Branch teams maintain business and employee data.
  3. Payroll, CRM, and Auditor ownership becomes active.

Execution and Review

  1. Branch and Contractor users submit operational evidence.
  2. CRM reviews filings, documents, renewals, and exceptions.
  3. Auditor performs audits and publishes reports.

Oversight and Resolution

  1. CCO monitors overdue and escalated portfolios.
  2. CEO reviews executive approvals and business-critical escalations.
  3. PF Team and Payroll close employee-facing issues.

Employee Experience

  1. Published payroll outputs become visible in ESS.
  2. Employees download payslips and documents.
  3. ESS helpdesk issues route to PF Team or Payroll.

Admin Module

Purpose: onboard clients, create users, assign owners, configure control data, and monitor system-wide operational health.

ADMIN

Daily Workflow

  1. Review dashboard KPIs, alerts, unread items, and escalations.
  2. Open Users to create, activate, deactivate, or correct role access.
  3. Open Clients to register new companies and maintain records.
  4. Create branches and configure applicability for each branch.
  5. Use Assignments and Payroll Assignments to map CRM, Auditor, and Payroll ownership.
  6. Review governance gaps, unassigned clients, and monitoring alerts.

Exception and Handoff

  • If a client is missing CRM, Auditor, or Payroll ownership, fix it before operations begin.
  • If users are inactive or mapped incorrectly, correct the user record immediately.
  • Handoff goes to Client, Branch, CRM, Auditor, and Payroll after setup is complete.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain client onboarding from user creation to assignments?
  • Can the trainee identify where governance gaps are cleared?

CEO Module

Purpose: review executive risk, approvals, escalations, reports, and enterprise-level registers.

CEO

Daily Workflow

  1. Open the dashboard and review executive KPIs and guardrails.
  2. Open Approvals for items requiring executive decision.
  3. Open Escalations for business-critical unresolved issues.
  4. Use Reports for period-based executive export packs.
  5. Use Registers for centralized output review.

Exception and Handoff

  • Rejected approvals move back to the previous operating owner.
  • Major unresolved escalations move downward again with direction for closure.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain when an escalation should remain with CCO and when it requires CEO action?

CCO Module

Purpose: supervise CRM teams, manage controls, review approvals, and close escalated compliance issues.

CCO

Daily Workflow

  1. Review the dashboard for pending approvals, CRM count, overdue tasks, and escalations.
  2. Use CRM performance tables to identify weak portfolios.
  3. Review Approvals and decide routed requests.
  4. Review Controls for SLA and escalation logic.
  5. Work Escalations and review Registers when output monitoring is needed.

Exception and Handoff

  • Corrective actions go back to CRM owners.
  • Repeated or unresolved risk items move to CEO.
  • Setup-related control gaps route to Admin.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain how controls affect escalation behavior?

CRM Module

Purpose: manage client portfolios, review branch submissions, process filings, coordinate audits, and track escalations.

CRM

Daily Workflow

  1. Start with the dashboard and due queues.
  2. Open All Clients to enter a client workspace.
  3. Use Document Review Center for approve versus reupload decisions.
  4. Use Returns / Filings to move filings through prepared, reviewed, filed, and acknowledged stages.
  5. Create and assign audits from Audits.
  6. Use SLA Tracker and Escalations when work remains overdue.

Exception and Handoff

  • Returned documents move back to Branch.
  • Contractor task or evidence issues move back to Contractor.
  • Persistent overdue issues move to CCO and then CEO if unresolved.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain the difference between review approval, reupload, and escalation?

Client Module

Purpose: review company-wide branches, employees, payroll readiness, approvals, and support follow-up.

CLIENT

Daily Workflow

  1. Review the dashboard with branch and period filters.
  2. Use Branches for organization-level branch visibility.
  3. Use Employees for employee master review and maintenance.
  4. Use Approvals Center for routed approvals.
  5. Use My Queries for support and follow-up.

Exception and Handoff

  • Branch execution issues move to Branch and CRM.
  • Payroll readiness issues move to Payroll.
  • Major support issues move through CRM or Admin channels.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee identify which branch needs attention and who should act next?

Branch Module

Purpose: execute monthly and periodic compliance uploads, respond to review remarks, and maintain branch-level operational data.

BRANCH

Daily Workflow

  1. Review branch KPIs, upload completion, and open observations on the dashboard.
  2. Use Monthly Compliance for monthly uploads and overdue items.
  3. Use Periodic Uploads for quarterly, half-yearly, and yearly submissions.
  4. Read and correct returned items, then resubmit.
  5. Use Helpdesk when branch execution is blocked.

Exception and Handoff

  • Submissions move to CRM for review.
  • Returned items come back to Branch for correction.
  • Overdue items can escalate upward through SLA rules.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain first submission versus reupload?

Auditor Module

Purpose: execute assigned audits, request reuploads, raise observations, and publish audit reports.

AUDITOR

Daily Workflow

  1. Review audit counts and due audits from the dashboard.
  2. Use Audits to open the next assigned workspace.
  3. Review scope, evidence, checklist, and branch context.
  4. Request reupload when evidence is insufficient.
  5. Raise observations, set severity, and complete report progress.
  6. Finalize outputs in Reports.

Exception and Handoff

  • Reupload requests go back to the source team.
  • Final outputs feed CRM, CCO, and CEO review cycles.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain when to request reupload and when to log an observation?

Contractor Module

Purpose: complete assigned tasks, upload evidence, answer reviewer remarks, and raise support tickets when blocked.

CONTRACTOR

Daily Workflow

  1. Open the dashboard and review required documents, in-progress items, and awaiting approval.
  2. Use Tasks to start, upload evidence, comment, and submit.
  3. Use Support for technical or compliance blockers.

Exception and Handoff

  • Rejected work returns for correction and resubmission.
  • Submitted work moves to the reviewer, usually CRM or another assigned team.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain when to use task comments versus a support ticket?

Payroll Module

Purpose: process client-scoped payroll runs, statutory outputs, reports, and related employee-facing results.

PAYROLL

Daily Workflow

  1. Review the payroll dashboard for active and pending runs.
  2. Open Clients and enter the target client workspace.
  3. Use Payroll Runs for input freeze, attendance import, arrears, preview, approval, and publishing.
  4. Use Reports and Registers for output generation.
  5. Use PF/ESI and queries pages when statutory or support issues exist.

Exception and Handoff

  • Published outputs feed ESS and business reporting.
  • Employee-facing PF, ESI, or payslip issues may route to PF Team.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee explain the difference between global payroll pages and client-scoped pages?

PF Team Module

Purpose: triage and resolve PF, ESI, and payslip-related support tickets from employees and payroll operations.

PF TEAM

Daily Workflow

  1. Review total, open, in-progress, and SLA-breached tickets from the dashboard.
  2. Use Tickets to filter by client, category, status, or priority.
  3. Open the ticket, review history, respond, and close when resolved.

Exception and Handoff

  • Issues needing payroll input are coordinated with Payroll.
  • Missing employee information must be collected before closure.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee identify which ticket must be handled first and why?

ESS Module

Purpose: let employees manage self-service actions such as payslips, documents, leave, profile, and helpdesk queries.

ESS

Daily Workflow

  1. Log in using company code, email, and password.
  2. Review the dashboard cards for payslip, PF, ESI, and leave balance.
  3. Use Payslips to filter and download payroll output.
  4. Use Documents to preview and download employee records.
  5. Use Leave, Nominations, My Profile, and Attendance for self-service updates.
  6. Use Helpdesk for PF, ESI, or payslip issues.

Exception and Handoff

  • Published payroll output comes from Payroll.
  • Helpdesk requests route to PF Team, Payroll, or HR support depending on issue type.

Trainer Check

  • Can the trainee independently download a payslip and locate a document?

Cross-Module Process Flows

New Client Setup

  1. Admin creates client, branches, and users.
  2. Admin assigns CRM, Auditor, and Payroll.
  3. Client and Branch start business data setup.

Branch to CRM Review

  1. Branch uploads compliance evidence.
  2. CRM approves or returns it for reupload.
  3. Branch corrects and resubmits if required.

Audit Lifecycle

  1. CRM creates the audit.
  2. Auditor executes the workspace and report.
  3. Completed outputs feed CRM, CCO, and CEO review.

Payroll to ESS

  1. Payroll processes and publishes a run.
  2. Payslips become visible in ESS.
  3. Employee issues route to PF Team or Payroll.

Escalation Chain

  1. CRM works the issue first.
  2. CCO intervenes if unresolved.
  3. CEO reviews severe or repeated cases.

Contractor Task Flow

  1. Task is assigned.
  2. Contractor uploads evidence and submits.
  3. Reviewer approves or rejects for correction.

Trainer Assessment Checklist

  1. Can the trainee explain the purpose of the role dashboard?
  2. Can the trainee identify the main daily work queue?
  3. Can the trainee explain what happens after approval?
  4. Can the trainee explain what happens after rejection?
  5. Can the trainee identify the next team in the process?
  6. Can the trainee explain when escalation is required?
